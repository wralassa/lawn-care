Early Spring (March - Early April)

Get Soil Test (Optional but Recommended)
MARBefore fertilizing
$15-20 at Madison County Extension Office (11775 US-231 N). Tests pH and nutrients. Bermuda likes 6.0-7.0 pH. Results in 1-2 weeks.
Spring Cleanup
MARAs soon as ground thaws
Rake up winter debris, dead grass, and leaves. This allows sunlight to reach the soil and warm it up faster.
Prep Your Mower
MARBefore greenup
Sharpen blades, change oil, fresh gas. Set mowing height to 1-2 inches for Bermuda.

Greenup Phase (Mid-April - May)

First Mow of Season
APRWhen grass reaches 2-3 inches
Scalp to 1 inch to remove dead material and encourage lateral growth. Bag the clippings.

Regular Mowing
MAY-SEPWeekly during active growth
Keep at 1-2 inches. Never remove more than 1/3 of blade height at once. Mow often!
Apply Grub Preventer
JUNLate May to mid-June
Prevents white grubs that damage Bermuda roots. Apply GrubEx or similar (contains chlorantraniliprole). Water in immediately. One application protects all season.
Deep Watering Routine
JUN-SEPDuring hot, dry periods
Huntsville avg: 4-5" rain in summer months. Supplement with 1-1.5" total per week. Water 2-3 times weekly (deep soaking), early morning 4-10am. Bermuda shows drought stress (blue-gray tint, footprints stay visible) before needing water.
Spot Treat Weeds
MAY-SEPAs needed
Hand pull or use post-emergent herbicide for broadleaf weeds (2,4-D, dicamba, or Image for tougher weeds). Spray when temps below 85¬∞F. Healthy, thick Bermuda crowds out most weeds naturally.
Core Aeration (If Needed)
JUNLate May to early June
Only needed if soil is compacted or heavy clay. Rent aerator or hire service (~$75-150 for 3,500 sq ft). Helps water/nutrients reach roots. Bermuda recovers quickly in active growth.
Overseed Bare Spots (Optional)
JUNJune when soil is warm
For thin areas: Use Bermuda seed or sprigs. Soil temp needs to be 65¬∞F+ consistently. Keep moist for 2-3 weeks. Bermuda also spreads naturally via runners.

Fall Preparation (October - November)

Final Mow & Cleanup
OCTBefore first frost
Last mow when grass stops growing (mid-late Oct). Cut to 1.5" to reduce snow mold risk. Remove all debris and fallen leaves.
Winterizer Application (Optional)
NOVEarly November
Optional 5th feeding with high-potassium "winterizer" fertilizer. Helps roots store energy for spring. Not critical for Bermuda but beneficial in Huntsville's climate.
